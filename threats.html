Types of Cyber Threats

Cyber threats are malicious activities designed to compromise the confidentiality, integrity, or availability of information systems. Understanding these threats is crucial for effective cybersecurity.

Common Types of Cyber Threats

1. Malware

Malware, short for malicious software, includes viruses, worms, Trojans, ransomware, and spyware. It is designed to damage, disrupt, or gain unauthorized access to systems.

2. Phishing

Phishing attacks involve tricking individuals into divulging sensitive information, such as usernames and passwords, often through fraudulent emails or websites.

3. Denial of Service (DoS) Attacks

DoS attacks aim to overwhelm a system, network, or website, rendering it unavailable to users by flooding it with traffic.

4. Man-in-the-Middle (MitM) Attacks

MitM attacks occur when an attacker intercepts and potentially alters communications between two parties without their knowledge.

5. SQL Injection

SQL Injection attacks exploit vulnerabilities in a website’s database query handling to execute malicious SQL statements.
