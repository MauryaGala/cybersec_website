What is Cybersecurity?

Cybersecurity refers to the practice of protecting systems, networks, and programs from digital attacks. These cyberattacks aim to access, change, or destroy sensitive information, extort money, or disrupt normal business processes. Implementing effective cybersecurity measures is challenging because there are more devices than people, and attackers are becoming more innovative.

Key Concepts in Cybersecurity

Confidentiality

Ensuring that information is accessible only to those authorized to have access. This includes protecting personal and sensitive information from unauthorized access.

Integrity

Maintaining the accuracy and completeness of data. Preventing unauthorized alterations or destruction of data is critical to ensure trustworthiness.

Availability

Ensuring that information and systems are accessible to authorized users when needed. Systems should be protected from disruptions caused by cyberattacks, such as denial-of-service (DoS) attacks.

Authentication & Non-repudiation

Authentication ensures that users are who they say they are, while non-repudiation ensures that actions taken cannot be denied later.
